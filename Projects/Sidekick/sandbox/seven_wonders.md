# Seven Wonders of the Ancient World

## Overview
The Seven Wonders of the Ancient World are a list of remarkable constructions known for their architectural and cultural significance in antiquity. Only one of them, the Great Pyramid of Giza, remains largely intact.

## The Seven Wonders

### 1. Great Pyramid of Giza
- **Built:** c. 2580–2560 BCE
- **Location:** Giza Plateau, Egypt
- **Description:** The oldest and largest of the Egyptian pyramids, originally standing at 146.6 meters.

### 2. Hanging Gardens of Babylon
- **Built:** c. 6th century BCE
- **Location:** Near present-day Hillah, Babylon, Iraq
- **Description:** A legendary terraced garden purportedly built by King Nebuchadnezzar II.

### 3. Statue of Zeus at Olympia
- **Built:** c. 435 BCE
- **Location:** Olympia, Greece
- **Description:** A giant seated figure of Zeus made by the sculptor Phidias.

### 4. Temple of Artemis at Ephesus
- **Built:** c. 550 BCE
- **Location:** Ephesus (near modern Selçuk, Turkey)
- **Description:** A massive marble temple dedicated to the goddess Artemis.

### 5. Mausoleum at Halicarnassus
- **Built:** c. 350 BCE
- **Location:** Bodrum, Turkey
- **Description:** Tomb of Mausolus, ruler of Caria.

### 6. Colossus of Rhodes
- **Built:** c. 292–280 BCE
- **Location:** Rhodes, Greece
- **Description:** A 33-meter tall bronze statue of the sun god Helios.

### 7. Lighthouse of Alexandria (Pharos)
- **Built:** c. 285–247 BCE
- **Location:** Alexandria, Egypt
- **Description:** An engineering marvel guiding ships into the harbor.

## Legacy
These wonders inspired later lists, including the New7Wonders of the World.
